\chapter{Game overview}
\textit{Mega Man X4} is the fourth installment in the Mega Man X series. Initially intended to be a Sega Saturn exclusive, the game was ultimately released on both the Sega Saturn and PlayStation in August and September 1997 in Japan and America, respectively. This made it the second game in the franchise to be available on these platforms, following its predecessor. The European version, however, was released exclusively for the PlayStation in October 1997. The game later became available for PC in Japan in December of the following year and in America and Europe in June 1999.

While retaining the series' core features, \textit{Mega Man X4} significantly changes its visual style, transitioning from a 16-bit to a fully 32-bit art style. Character sprites were entirely redesigned, nearly doubling the total number of animations~\cite{wikip:X4}, and the quality of the backgrounds was also enhanced. Due to the large number of sprites, the developers implemented a compression routine to fit everything, which resulted in almost all stages being split into two sections. Additionally, this is the first game in the series to feature fully voiced FMV cutscenes, although its predecessor had non-voiced ones. Notably, the game also marks the first time that Zero is fully playable, offering a radically different playstyle and a dedicated campaign compared to X.

Overall, the game was praised for the changes it introduced to the series, particularly the addition of Zero and the updated graphics. However, it faced criticism for its limited innovation in gameplay compared to its predecessor and, most notably, for the poor quality of the voice acting, which is still remembered today. Interestingly, not all the game's elements received adaptation, as the eight bosses' battle quote kept their Japanese voice acting (this does not apply to later bosses) and their pre-battle dialogue with the player characters were completely removed.

\section{Main plot}

In the year 21XX, the security of humans and reploids against Maverick attacks is ensured through the combined efforts of the Maverick Hunters and the Reploid Force, also known as \textit{Repliforce}. Like the Maverick Hunters, Repliforce is a military organization composed of reploids, with dedicated soldiers and scientists working to counter Maverick threats. However, unlike the Maverick Hunters, Repliforce follows a strict, military-based structure, consisting of soldiers ranked up to General, its supreme commander, and organized similarly to a traditional army, divided into forces such as land, air, and marine, all with the task of dealing with large-scale emergencies. However, following the failure of Sigma's leadership and Doppler's vaccine to stop the mavericks, Dr.~Cain decides to reorganize Repliforce into a v3 version. This new structure aims to better support the Maverick Hunters in combating mavericks by reducing response times to attacks, improving scientific collaboration between the Maverick Hunters and Repliforce scientists, and maximizing reploid efficiency~\cite{X4:Manual}. Still, six months after the reorganization, an evaluation shows that the operation has not been successful: the response times of the Maverick Hunters have not improved, and maverick attacks have increased at an alarming rate. As a result, Repliforce v3 is deemed ineffective and, also, potentially dangerous. Consequently, Cain decides to restructure the Maverick Hunters into a new V4 version, merging the 17th and 0th Units, led by X and Zero, respectively. This V4 reorganization proves successful, as the newly formed unit effectively responds to maverick attacks in the same capacity as Repliforce.

It is within this context that Mega Man X4 begins. The story opens with a revived and disguised Sigma approaching General, Repliforce's supreme commander, and attempting to persuade him. Sigma warns General about the danger the Maverick Hunters pose to all reploids, claiming that any reploid who defies humans is labeled as a maverick and therefore hunted. Sigma urges General to preemptively strike, asserting that it’s only a matter of time before the Hunters come for Repliforce. However, despite Sigma’s attempts, General dismisses his warnings and Sigma leaves, although with a smile, having sown doubt in General’s mind. Next, Sigma proceeds with his plan.l First, he manipulates Magma Dragoon, a Maverick Hunter, by exploiting his obsession with facing X in combat. Under Sigma's order, Dragoon leads an attack on the flying city of Sky Lagoon using a giant mechaniloid, inciting chaos and drawing Repliforce into battle~\cite{wiki:repliforce_war}. Maverick Hunters X and Zero are also dispatched, unaware of Repliforce’s presence, but when they arrive, Dragoon sabotages the city's flight systems, causing Sky Lagoon to crash with catastrophic casualties. X and Zero navigate the wreckage to eliminate the mechaniloid, with Zero rescuing Iris in the process. After the battle, Colonel,Repliforce’s second-in-command and Iris’s brother, arrives to protect her. Suspicious of Repliforce’s involvement, X and Zero ask Colonel to disarm and come with them, suspecting him of being a maverick, but Colonel refuses due to his pride as a soldier, even if it means being branded a Maverick. Consequently, Repliforce is blamed for Sky Lagoon's destruction, and its members are labeled Mavericks. Following these accusations, General declares Repliforce’s independence from humans, launching a coup to create a reploid-only nation for reploids’ freedom and safety.

X and Zero subsequently set out to stop Repliforce’s rebellion, X being supported by rookie-hunter Double, while Iris provides support to Zero. The pair manage to stop various mavericks attacks, and each one even face Colonel in a duel. X ultimately defeats Colonel, while Iris intervenes to prevent the confrontation between Zero and her brother, reminding Colonel of his debt to Zero. Once the primary Maverick threats are neutralized, X and Zero learn that Repliforce’s ships are leaving Earth for space, aiming to set up a colony in space where they can create their own nation. X and Zero therefore rush to the spaceport, where Colonel stays behind to delay the Hunters and buy his soldiers more time to escape, but after a fierce battle, is defeated and ultimately dies. From here X continues to pursue Repliforce, while Zero briefly returns to base, only to find that Iris has left to join Repliforce, devastated by her brother’s death. At the same time, Double reveals himself as Sigma’s spy, killing two Hunters before pursuing X into the Final Weapon, Repliforce’s space colony and supposed utopia.

Upon reaching their destination the couple receive a bitter welcome, as X encounters Double who starts mocking him for his trust in others, while Zero finds Iris who, overcome with grief for her brother's death and decided to fulfill Colonel's wish, merges with Colonel’s core and attacks Zero. Both X and Zero succeed in defeating their opponents, though Zero remains heartbroken, as the damage he inflicted on Iris proves to be fatal. In her final moments, Iris expresses her dream of a world where she, Zero, and her brother could have lived peacefully and happily, leaving Zero questioning the purpose of his battles. Despite their sorrow, X and Zero press on, ultimately confronting and defeating General in hopes of stopping the coup. However, even with General defeated, the Final Weapon begins to move independently, aiming his arsenal toward Earth and revealing the presence of a hidden operator. This figure reveals himself to be Sigma, who faces off with the Maverick Hunters once more. After a fierce battle the couple emerge victorious, but despite Sigma's defeated, the weapon’s firing process remains active, leaving X and Zero uncertain of their next move. in this final moment General reappears, seeking forgiveness for Repliforce’s foolishness. General decides to sacrifice himself, using his damaged body to intercept the weapon’s blast and disabling it permanently. As the weapon collapses and explodes, X and Zero escape, each one carrying their own burdens: X questions the nature of a ``Maverick'' and makes Zero promise to end him if he ever becomes one; Zero, haunted by Iris and Sigma’s revelations, wonders about his true identity.

\section{Main Characters}

\subsection{X}
One of the main protagonist of the game, X (more detail in chapter~\ref{char:X}) He is the leader of the Maverick Hunters' 17th Elite Unit and a well-renowned Maverick Hunter, especially since he was responsible having defeated Sigma thrice in the past. X takes very seriously his task of protecting humans and reploids, but this clashes with his pacifist heart and benevolent spirit which refuses violence. This cause an inner conflict and a trouble very few people can understand.

X's origins are considered a mystery, as nobody knows why he was built or buried away~\cite{X4:Manual}.

\subsection{Zero}
The second protagonist of the game, Zero is an incredibly skilled SA-class maverick Hunter, leader of the special 0th unit and X's best friend. Unlikely X, Zero has a cold attitude, never hesitating to carry on his duty, and this makes him the perfect partner for X, especially since Zero is one of the very few people who seems to understand X's struggle~\cite{Xcoll1:Manual_X4}.

Just like X, Zero's origins are surrounded by mystery.

In the events of Mega Man X4, Repliforce is framed responsible for the disaster in Sky Lagoon, having been lured in by Magma Dragoon's attack and refusing to disarm when Maverick Hunters requested to  follow for interrogations. \section{Game Mechanics}

Following the path set by its predecessors, Mega Man X4 continues to innovate its gameplay by introducing new features and modifying existing ones to keep the gameplay new yet familiar to long-time fans of the series. The main features in Mega Man X4 are as follows:
\begin{itemize}
	\item \textbf{Fully Playable Zero}: As an improvement over X3’s ``Zero Change'' feature, Mega Man X4 offers a fully playable Zero. Zero has his own dedicated campaign mirroring X’s own, and possesses his own \textit{learning system}, mimicking X's variable weapons system, which allow to obtain unique techniques from defeated bosses and collectible items. However, Zero cannot acquire any armor parts, as these are exclusive to X.
	\begin{figure}[htp]
		\centering
		\includegraphics[height=3cm]{figures/X4/Choose_2.png}
		\caption{Character selection screen}
	\end{figure}
	\item \textbf{Sub Tank System Change}: Before Mega Man X4, collectible sub tanks consisted of four hidden sub tanks across the stages that could be filled by picking up energy drops from enemies on a one-to-one ratio, as long as the player’s health was full. Mega Man X4 modifies this system by reducing the total number of sub tanks to two and allowing them to be recharged each time a health pickup is collected, regardless of the player’s health. However, they are refilled by a fixed amount rather than the amount the player’s health would have restored~\cite{wiki:sub_tank}. Additionally, X4 compensates for the loss of two sub tanks by introducing two new types of sub tanks: a weapon sub tank, which functions similarly to the life tank, and the EX-item, which increases the starting lives from two to four when beginning a stage.
	\item The pink armor capsule system from the previous game has been removed.
	\item Similarly, the Ride Armor system has been changed back to the way it was in the X1-X2 games, with the removal of summoning platforms and upgrade modules.
	\item \textbf{Ride Chaser Return}: The Ride Chaser vehicles from Mega Man X2 make a return. However, unlike in X2 where they functioned more like Ride Armors, in this game, the Ride Chaser is featured in a high-speed, auto-scrolling section limited to a single stage.
	\item \textbf{Dual Fire Button}: Thanks to upgraded hardware, the game allows players to map two buttons to the fire command, one for the regular X-Buster and another for the sub-weapon. This enables players to use both the regular fire and sub-weapon at the same time, unlike previous games where only one could be used at once. However, it’s not possible to charge one while using the other, as firing a sub-weapon interrupts the charge of the main weapon and vice-versa.
	\item \textbf{Two-part stages}: A new element for this game consists in having most of the stages divided into two big areas, often differentiated by enemies inside and background/environment. Reaching the second area of a stage also act as an intermediate check point, giving players the choice to restart from the beginning of it should they receive a game-over.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{\texorpdfstring{\includegraphics[width=10px, height=10px]{figures/X4/weapons/Ico_web.png} Lightning Web}{Lightning Web}}\label{Lightning_Web}
Obtained after defeating Web Spider(\ref{boss:Web_spider}), the Lightning Web fires a spider net charged with electricity that can be used to attack, wall jump, or double jump. Upon firing, X will shoot an electric projectile that travels a fixed distance before opening up. In this state, the web behaves like a wall for X to jump and wall-jump on, lasting for a short amount of time. Once X jumps onto it, the web disappears shortly after, regardless of the residual time. Additionally, this weapon deals damage only in its projectile form.

Upon charging, X fires a lightning web that subsequently opens up to eight more, covering a large portion of the screen and dealing continuous damage over time. This web, however, is non-solid and cannot be climbed on.

\subsection{\texorpdfstring{\includegraphics[width=10px, height=10px]{figures/X4/weapons/Ico_frost.png} Frost Tower}{Frost Tower}}\label{Frost_Tower}
When using the Frost Tower, X generates a large ice block that acts as a shield, which can also trap enemies before dissipating. Upon activation, X creates an ice block around him that remains stationary on the ground, serving as both an obstacle for enemies and a shield for X. Once activated, X can move freely since the ice block is not a solid obstacle for him, meaning he can hide behind it while shooting regularly with the X-Buster. Enemies, however, take contact damage from the ice  (4 damages every 9 frames~\cite{wiki:X4_enemies}), which lasts for a short time before shattering. Enemy projectiles, however, are not stopped by it. 

When used in the air, X briefly stops mid-air to create the ice block, which will promptly fall to the ground. Only one ice block can be present on the screen at a time; creating a second one causes the previous block to disappear.

When charged, this weapon causes various giant ice shards to fall from the sky in four waves, with an increasing number of falling shards: one, two, three, and four, for a total of ten shards. As with the regular version, X can move freely once the first wave begins, and the shards follow him as he moves.

This weapon is obtained only after X has defeated Frost Walrus(\ref{boss:Frost_Walrus}).

\subsection{\texorpdfstring{\includegraphics[width=10px, height=10px]{figures/X4/weapons/Ico_soul.png} Soul Body}{Soul Body}}\label{Soul_Body}
Soul Body is the weapon X obtains after defeating Split Mushroom (\ref{boss:Split_Mushroom}). This weapon accumulates energy to generate an illusion, and enemies will sustain damage if struck by the soul energy. When activated, X creates a hologram of his silhouette in front of him that mimics his movements, remaining at a fixed distance and always positioned in front of X. If X turns, the copy will automatically reposition in front of him at the same distance. The hologram deals contact damage to enemies (2 damage every 9 frames~\cite{wiki:X4_enemies}) and dissipates after a certain amount of time or if it receives enough damage. Only one clone can be generated at a time.

When charged, X creates an unarmored clone of himself that the player can control. This clone can move only within the current screen and can fire using the regular, uncharged X-Buster. Moreover, it is immune to all damage and disappears only after a short period of time or if it falls into a pit. While controlling the clone, X remains frozen in the location where he created it (even if in mid-air), but he is immune to all damage.

\subsection{\texorpdfstring{\includegraphics[width=10px, height=10px]{figures/X4/weapons/Ico_Rising.png} Rising Fire}{Rising Fire}}\label{Rising_Fire}
When equipping the Rising Fire, X shoots a fireball straight upward, with unlimited vertical range but minimal horizontal reach. When fired in the air, X briefly stops mid-air, extending his airborne time. This weapon also has a fast firing rate, allowing X to shoot it multiple times in quick succession. 

When charged, Rising Fire causes X to rise into the air, performing a flaming uppercut in a Shoryuken-like manner, ending with a fireball release similar to the regular version. Performing the charged version in mid-air allows X to perform a second jump, increasing his vertical reach.

Rising Fire is the weapon X obtains after defeating Magma Dragoon (\ref{boss:Magma_dragoon}).

\subsection{\texorpdfstring{\includegraphics[width=10px, height=10px]{figures/X4/weapons/Ico_hunter.png} Ground Hunter}{Ground Hunter}}\label{Ground_Hunter}
Acquired after defeating Jet Stingray (\ref{boss:Jet_Stingray}), the Ground Hunter fires small stingray drones that race across the ground, making it particularly effective against enemies on walls and floors. When firing normally, X can deploy up to three drones that glide down from the buster to the ground, then proceed to move forward, following the ground and even descending along walls. By pressing down while the stingray drones are in flight, players can command them to make an immediate 90-degree turn downward. This command applies to all drones on screen regardless of when they were fired, and firing with the down direction already pressed causes the drones to launch vertically.

When charged, X fires a single, larger drone that travels straight forward. Players can press up or down on the gamepad to release additional energy drones above and below as the main drone moves forward.

\subsection{\texorpdfstring{\includegraphics[width=10px, height=10px]{figures/X4/weapons/Ico_aiming.png} Aiming Laser}{Aiming Laser}}\label{Aiming_Laser}

Defeating Cyber Peacock (\ref{boss:Cyber_peacock}) grants X access to the Aiming Laser. Equipping this weapon causes a cursor to appear on the screen in front of X, which players can control by pressing up or down on the gamepad to move it along its path. When the cursor moves over an enemy, it locks onto that enemy, allowing X to fire a laser aimed directly at it, regardless of position. The cursor can lock onto up to three enemies at a time, and three lasers of different colors (green, yellow, and red) will simultaneously fire at the locked targets without additional energy cost.

When charged, the weapon causes a fan-shaped laser to emit from the X-Buster for a short duration. This charged laser can be controlled similarly to the cursor in the regular version, dealing contact damage to all enemies within its range.

\section{Zero's Techniques}\label{X4:sub_weapon_Z}

\subsection{\texorpdfstring{\includegraphics[width=10px, height=10px]{figures/X4/weapons/Zero_1.png} Rakuhouha}{Rakuhouha}}\label{Rakuhouha}

Defeating Cyber Peacock (\ref{boss:Cyber_peacock}) grants Zero access to Rakuhouha, a powerful attack that channels all stored energy into a single blast. When pressing the Giga Attack button while grounded, Zero punches the ground and shouts ``Get Ready'' before releasing nine bolts of energy that disperse in a fan shape, destroying most enemies in a single hit and even passing through walls.

This weapon is the only one that consumes Zero's energy meter. Due to its high damage output and range, it is also one of the most costly weapons in the game, depleting the energy meter in only four uses. However, this weapon restores some energy each time Zero is hit~\cite{wiki:Rakuhouha}.

\section{Fourth Armor}\label{X4:Armor}
Following the tradition established by previous games, Mega Man X4 introduces a new set of armor parts split into four different capsules hidden across distinct stages. However, unlike previous entries, Mega Man X4 breaks the tradition of having a secret fifth capsule obtainable by collecting all hidden collectibles. Obviously, the capsule system is exclusive to X's campaign, as armor capsules do not appear at all during Zero's campaign.

The Fourth Armor, also referred to in different sources as the \textit{Normal Armor}~\cite{book:Compendium} or \textit{Force Armor}~\cite{X5:Manual}, consists of the following parts:
\begin{figure}[htp]

	\caption{The Fourth Armor}
\end{figure}

\begin{itemize}
	\item \emph{Foot Parts:} These allow X to perform an air dash, similar to previous games. Additionally, by pressing the jump button a second time while in the air, X can hover in place for a few seconds. While hovering, X can move slightly forward or backward without turning, though moving significantly reduces hover time. The capsule containing the Foot Parts is hidden in Web Spider's stage, in a side path behind a waterfall near the beginning.
	
	\item \emph{Body Parts:} Found in Magma Dragoon's stage, near the end of the second section and where the Ride Armor can be found, hidden behind a wall breakable only by using a charged Twin Slasher. These parts reduce all incoming damage of approximately 33\%~\cite{wiki:X4_enemies} and convert it into energy for the Nova Strike—an invincible, high-damage air dash executed by pressing the Giga-Attack button.

Hidden in Storm Owl's stage, these parts are located in a secret path accessible only by using the Lightning Web before the stage's mini-boss. Uniquely, players will find two capsules and can choose one of two versions of this upgrade, which can be swapped any time by revisiting the other capsule:
	\begin{itemize}
		\item \emph{Stock Charged Shot:} Allows X to store up to four smaller charged shots, which are displayed as icons under X's health bar. Pressing the shoot button fires a stored charged shot instead of a regular one if available. The Arm Parts for this version are white and blue.

\section{Opening Stage: Sky Lagoon}\label{X4:intro_stage}
Sky Lagoon is the first stage players encounter in the game, regardless of whether they choose X or Zero. The stage is split into two distinct halves: the first takes place in the floating city of Sky Lagoon, which is under attack by Maverick forces, while the second occurs in the ruins after the city's catastrophic fall. As with any introductory stage, this level serves as a tutorial to teach players the basic game mechanics.

The first area is a linear section where the stage boss, Eregion, intermittently attacks from the background or foreground, attacking with either its claws or by firing plasma shots. Players must progress while evading these attacks and dealing with shut doors, which can either be destroyed or jumped over, except for the final door, which has to be destroyed, causing Eregion to flee. Interestingly, players can also defeat Eregion by attacking its claws when it strikes, forcing it to retreat earlier but without any significant consequences. After Eregion retreats, players traverse a long corridor filled with enemies, eventually reaching the generator room of Sky Lagoon. Here, X or Zero encounters Magma Dragoon, who declares that Sky Lagoon is doomed to fall before escaping. X or Zero are forced to teleport away as the floating city crashes to the ground.

The ruins of Sky Lagoon provide the setting for the second half of the stage. The primary threats here include falling debris, which can crush X or Zero, and \hyperlink{miniboss:trap_blast}{Trap Blast} enemies, which emerge from the debris. Near the end of the stage, the floor collapses, leading to a pit containing collectibles that requires players to wall-jump to escape. However, skilled players who move quickly can avoid falling into the pit altogether, reaching the next section where Eregion, the stage boss, awaits.

\section{Cyberspace}
The Cyberspace stage sees X and Zero traveling through a cybernetic maze in order to reach its deepest part and defeat Cyber Peacock.  

Just like other stages, this one also is split into two sections. The first revolves around time trials, where players must navigate three separate areas, racing to reach the end in the shortest amount of time: the first area consists of a corridor filled with obstacles that players must jump over, the second is a large room divided into three levels, connected by gaps that allow movement across layers, and the third is a vertical room where players must climb using ladders or wall jumps.  
Throughout these sections, enemies also spawn to hinder the players, especially \hyperlink{enem:Spike_Marl}{Spike Marls}, which appear through portals and roll towards X and Zero but which can be easily avoided by dashing underneath the spawn portal, and the \hyperlink{Miru_Toraeru}{Miru Toraeru}, a much bigger threat which traps players and slows them down. These enemies are either stationary or roll towards the player, strategically positioned to block certain passages. They are also immune to all attacks except X's Soul Body and Zero's Kuuenzan.  

At the end of each section, players receive a score ranging from S to B, depending on the time taken to complete the trial. A portal will then open, leading to different parts of the stage based on the rank obtained. Achieving an S rank grants access to a bonus area where a collectible can be found, depending on the completed trial; rank A allows players to proceed to the next section; rank B instead forces them to repeat the trial. Although players cannot see their score directly, the cursor following X and Zero will occasionally display a downward red arrow, indicating that the score has decreased.  

The second section takes a slower approach, replacing time trials with puzzles involving \hyperlink{Protecton}{Protectons} and switches that flip the path upside down. The key to progressing is to activate the switches strategically, causing Protectons to roll down and break otherwise indestructible barriers while avoiding direct collisions to prevent damage. Once past this section, players will reach the boss room, where Cyber Peacock awaits.  

This stage is probably the one with less enemy variety, housing only the five kind of enemies listed below~\cite{wiki:Cyberspace}, only three of which will try to actively hurt the player.

	\item \hyperlink{enem:Protecton}{Protecton}

\end{itemize}

The battle against Cyber Peacock primarily tests players' reflexes. During the fight, he constantly teleports near the player and executes one of his two main attacks: the \emph{Feather Attack} or the \emph{Rising Slash}. In the first, Peacock extends his feathers in all directions after teleporting near the player, covering a considerable range around him. In the second, he quickly ascends, sacrificing horizontal coverage for increased vertical reach. Once he has taken damage at least once~\cite{wiki:Cyber_peacock}, Peacock will also start using his \emph{Tracking Shot}. In this attack, he teleports to either the left or right side of the arena and locks onto X with a crosshair before firing homing feathers. These projectiles move relatively fast but disappear after chasing X for a short duration.  

Facing Cyber Peacock early in the game can be particularly challenging due to the high damage output and speed of his attacks, capable of defeating unequipped players in just two or three hits. Without exploiting his weakness, the best strategy is to take advantage of the brief interval between his appearance on screen and the execution of his attack, striking as soon as he materializes and retreating immediately to avoid damage. Another key opportunity arises when Peacock uses his Tracking Shot, as he remains stationary for a short period. However, players must attack while also evading the homing feathers. Between X and Zero, the former has a clear advantage due to his ability to attack from a distance, whereas Zero must expose himself to greater risk.  The situation changes drastically when fighting Peacock using his weakness, which differs depending on the character. For X, the weakness is the Soul Body, while for Zero, it is the Ryuenjin. In both cases, hitting Peacock with his respective weakness causes him to static out and immediately teleport away, effectively removing any challenge from the fight, since he won't be able to perform any other attack,  due to lacking invincibility frames as he reappears, meaning it is possible to force him in a death loop just by striking him as soon as he appears on screen.

\section{Air Force}
The third stage available in the game is the Air Force stage. Set in the sky, this stage follows X and Zero as they battle Repliforce's air fleet, aiming to reach the main ship and take down Storm Owl, the fleet's commander.  

The first half of the stage consists primarily of platforming across airships while avoiding numerous enemies that attempt to knock X and Zero into the pits below. Among these foes, \hyperlink{enem:Metal_Hawk}{Metal Hawks} can also serve as temporary platforms, though they will still attack  using their missiles. About one-third into the stage, players will encounter the \hyperlink{vehicle:Ride_Armor_Eagle}{Ride Armor Eagle}, a ride armor equipped with thrusters that enable hovering, air dashing, and firing homing energy shots, providing significant help in navigating the remainder of the stage. However, while the Ride Armor can hover indefinitely, taking damage from an enemy will cause it to fall, which can be particularly dangerous when hovering over a pit.  The rest of this first section alternates between sequences featuring flying platforms—destroyed by large lasers firing from above as the player approaches—and a segment populated by multiple \hyperlink{enem:Plasma_Cannon}{Plasma Cannons}.  

The second half of the stage takes place within a single airship, which players must traverse to reach the boss. Here, \hyperlink{enem:Beam_Cannons}{Beam Cannons} swarm the area, firing lasers that leave only small spaces for players to maneuver through. The most dangerous part of this section is a spiked pit near the beginning, which, when combined with the aforementioned enemies, can become a deadly trap. Past the spiked section, players will encounter the stage's sub-boss, the \hyperlink{miniboss:Generaid_Core}{Generaid Core}.  
Unlike most enemies, the Generaid Core does not actively attack; instead, it remains stationary, occasionally exposing its red core—its only vulnerable spot—while summoning waves of Beam Cannons to fill the room in an attempt to hit the player. The best strategies for quickly defeating the miniboss include using Zero's Ryuenjin or X's Lightning Web. Additionally, a well-timed charged Aiming Laser can continuously damage the core upon impact, potentially destroying it before it has the chance to hide itself again.  

Defeating the Generaid Core will cause an opening to appear in the right wall, leading directly to Storm Owl's location.

Throughout his boss battle, Storm Owl primarily flies around the arena, following a relatively straightforward pattern of attacking, disappearing off-screen, and reappearing to unleash another move from his arsenal. His first option is the \emph{swoop} attack, where he grabs the player with his talons and slams them into the ground for significant damage. Alternatively, he can reappear from one of the arena’s corners to use his \emph{3-way shot}, launching a spread of feather projectiles in three directions—either upward or downward, depending on his position. These feathers can be destroyed with standard attacks, and Owl ceases using this move once his health falls below 50\%. Another notable move is his \emph{Cyclone Attack}, which comes in three variations: if X or Zero is on the central platform, Owl flies to the middle and fires a single cyclone at the player; if the player remains on the ground, he scatters four cyclones that home in simultaneously. \section{Volcano}\label{volcano}

Magma Dragoon’s fight is a true test of agility and pattern recognition. Most of his attacks can be avoided either by dashing underneath them or jumping over them, as Dragoon relentlessly cycles through his moves in predictable patterns. His first attack, the \emph{Hadouken}, is reminiscent of the iconic move from the Street Fighter series, with Dragoon firing a fireball horizontally. When executed while standing, the fireball must be dodged by dashing underneath it, whereas a crouching Hadoken requires players to jump over it. His second move, the \emph{Rising Fire}, is a blazing uppercut attack he performs after shouting \textit{Shoryuken} which covers a significant vertical and horizontal area and can only be avoided by quickly dashing beneath Dragoon as he leaps upward or dashing away from him. Additionally, Dragoon can also employ his \emph{Nosedive}, a rapid diving kick performed after a brief hop; this is his fastest move and can be avoided by dashing away. Beyond close-range techniques, Dragoon also utilizes a series of ranged attacks to control the battlefield and create deadly traps: the \emph{Fire Pillar Attack} causes flames to erupt from one side of the arena, making the adjacent wall useless for wall-jumping; the \emph{Fire Attack} sees Dragoon unleashing a stream of fire across the entire horizontal span of the arena, leaving only the walls and a narrow strip of ground behind him and the walls safe; finally, the \emph{Flame Shot} summons meteors that rain diagonally from the top right, forcing players to retreat to the top left corner for safety. As previously mentioned, quick reflexes and memorization are essential to defeating Magma Dragoon. It is critical to understand that Dragoon rarely performs isolated attacks; instead, he chains together sequences before shifting to another pattern. Quickly recognizing these sequences is crucial, allowing players to dodge effectively and capitalize on the openings each attack provides, as many of his moves leave him vulnerable for counterattacks. Positioning also plays a crucial role: the edges of the arena are lined with deadly lava, further restricting movement and punishing players who over-rely on wall jumping to evade Dragoon's assaults. During the first phase of the battle, Dragoon often initiates combat with a Nosedive to close the distance, followed by one of two possible combinations: either two consecutive Hadoken, one high and one low, or a crouching Hadoken followed by a Rising Fire. Alternatively, if Dragoon summons a fire pillar, he will always follow it up with two Rising Fires. In the latter half of the battle, Dragoon modifies his combos, unleashing three Hadoken in succession—high, low, then high again. His other attacks are in between these combos to catch players off guard. Among all his moves, the Fire Attack presents perhaps the best opportunity for retaliation, as Dragoon remains stationary for a significant period, allowing players to jump behind him and deliver damage. Despite the seeming difficulty of the fight, players have access to several advantages to ease the battle. The Ride Armor which can be brought into the encounter provides invaluable assistance by rendering the player temporarily invincible and allowing a rapid and heavy damage output. Furthermore, in X’s case, using Dragoon’s weakness—the Double Cyclone—greatly simplifies the battle, as striking Dragoon with it interrupts his attack pattern and forces him to leap to the opposite side of the arena (as shown in file \path{videos/X4/Dragoon_loop.mp4}). Unfortunately, Zero lacks such an advantage; while his strongest technique, the Raijingeki, deals substantial damage, it offers no additional effects beyond raw power and the long animation, combined with the hit-stop caused by Dragoon's invincibility frames, lock Zero in place and expose him a lot to Dragoon's attacks.

\section{Marine Base} 
The Marine Base Stage is almost certainly the most unique in the game, as it presents itself as an expansion of the Ride Chaser sections introduced in the second installment of the series, rather than following the structure of a traditional run-and-gun level. The stage, in fact, consists entirely of a single auto-scrolling section in which the player controls the Ride Chaser. The vehicle is limited to three basic actions: jumping, firing, and dashing forward, the latter option which can be executed either on the ground or in mid-air; in the latter case, the Ride Chaser will briefly hover before falling back down. The dash, while being a powerful offensive move that can destroy any obstacle in a single hit, is a high-risk, high-reward maneuver, as mistiming it may cause the player's death by falling into a pit or crashing into an obstacle, due to the dash’s relatively long cooldown period and the inability to move immediately after.

The first part of the stage tasks the player to drive the Ride Chaser through a tunnel, throughout which enemy Ride Chasers will approach, attempting to hit into the player. Additionally, mines are scattered across the path, requiring well-timed jumps to avoid damage, and the terrain features numerous pits and slopes, requiring sharp reflexes and quick reactions. Near the end of this sequence, the tunnel begins to collapse, with explosions and flames rapidly coming from behind. Simultaneously, metallic barriers appear as new obstacles, that must be either shot down or destroyed by dashing through them. While dashing is the more effective method, it also carries greater risk—especially since the barriers often appear in rapid succession. Prematurely initiating a dash can leave the player stranded behind an intact barrier with no time to recover, potentially resulting in a loss.

Upon escaping the tunnel, which explodes shortly after the player exits, the second portion of the stage begins, now set in an outdoor environment. Here, obstacles become more frequent and dangerous, as the level design makes use of steel scaffolding coming from both the ground and ceiling to punish players who abuse the air dash. To further increase the difficulty, the stage boss, Jet Stingray, also appears to interfere with the player's progress, by flying ahead of the player and releases projectiles while moving. Fortunately, it is possible to make him flee by dashing directly into him, which forces him to retreat immediately. During this outdoor segment, Stingray appears twice in total, but in subsequent playthroughs after his defeat he will no longer appears. Upon reaching the end of the route, X or Zero will automatically dismount the Ride Chaser, leaving the player only with the task of walking through the boss door.

\section{Bio Laboratory}
Among the eight stages, the Bio Laboratory is arguably the largest in terms of navigation time, featuring an impressive total of seven distinct sections—four in the first half and three in the second. In this level, X and Zero venture into a long-abandoned research facility to investigate the cause of its sudden reactivation.

The first half of the stage is divided into four different sections: the opening segment consists of a spiral staircase filled with hostile enemies; following is a more traditional platforming segment suspended above bottomless pits; the third section involves a vertical climb, during which invincible \hyperlink{enem:Tentoroid_BS}{Tentoroid BS} enemies ram into the player from outside the tower walls; finally, the fourth segment culminates in a battle against the miniboss \hyperlink{miniboss:Tentoroid}{Tentoroid}, which attacks by moving back and forth before aligning itself with the player's position and propelling upward. This action destroys the floor tiles above and reveals a spiked pit, placing the player under a time limit to defeat the boss. The only opportunities to damage Tentoroid occur when it rises to attack, making upward strikes like Zero's \emph{Ryuenjin} especially effective. Additionally, attacks that can penetrate the floor, such as Zero’s \emph{Hyouretsuzan} and X’s \emph{Aiming Laser}, \emph{Ground Hunter}, and \emph{Frost Tower} can also hit the boss, although the latter two require at least one broken tile for the attack to pass through. Alternatively, it is possible to skip entirely the battle, by performing the glitch shown in section~\ref{x4_misc_mushroom_skip}.

The second half of the stage mirrors the first in structure, only with slight variations in enemy placement. It begins with another staircase section, this time featuring a \hyperlink{enem:Death_Guardian}{Death Guardian} relentlessly chasing the player. The next section is another platforming area, complicated by the return of the \hyperlink{enem:Tentoroid_BS}{Tentoroid BS}, which now destroy portions of the stage, causing them to gradually collapse into the pit below. The final part is an elevator ride where \hyperlink{enem:Togerics}{Togerics} suddenly sprout up to become crushing obstacles. Upon reaching the top of the tower, the player finally arrives at the arena where Split Mushroom awaits

\section{Military Train} 
Home to Slash Beast, the Military Train stage follows X and Zero as they navigate a heavily armed supply convoy, attempting to reach the front of the train and bring it to a halt.

The stage plays like a traditional Mega Man level, with players moving from one wagon to the next while destroying crates and defeating enemies positioned on top of them. On three occasions during the stage, a \hyperlink{enem:Knot_Berets_B}{Knot Berets B} appears and destroys the connection between wagons. However, this situation is easily resolved with a quick jump onto the next car. At the end of the first section, the players’ wagon is approached from behind by the \hyperlink{enem:DG-42L}{DG-42L} miniboss, which starts attacking. This enemy follows a simple attack pattern, extending its spikes one at a time and occasionally firing a cannon from its top. To defeat it, players must destroy all of its weapons, keeping in mind that the shafts of the spikes do not deal damage and are safe to stand in between.

Once the miniboss is defeated, players proceed to the second section of the stage, which takes place inside a tunnel. Here, they are provided with a Ride Armor, which can be used to destroy enemies, heavy crates, and even parts of the wagons themselves. After progressing for a while, players reach the head of the train, which comes to a stop. At this point, they must exit the Ride Armor and cross a series of pits to board the next train, which begins moving as soon as they land on it. From there, players have only a short distance to travel before arriving at the final wagon, where Slash Beast will attack them.

\section{Snow Base}
The final stage available to players is the Snow Base, where X and Zero venture into an icy cavern in search of Repliforce captain Frost Walrus. As in previous entries in the series, this stage centers around slippery terrain, leading to hazardous spikes and bottomless pits.
\begin{figure}[htp]
	\centering
	\includegraphics[height=3cm]{figures/X4/Frost_Walrus/walrus_buffalo.png}
	\includegraphics[height=3cm]{figures/X4/Frost_Walrus/walrus_penguin.png}

At the very beginning of the stage, near a statue of a frozen Blizzard Buffalo (\ref{boss:Blizzard_buffalo}), players are presented with two possible paths. The upper path, accessible by wall-climbing and only traversable if players have previously obtained either the Kuuenbu or the Lightning Web, contains numerous item pickups and fewer enemies. The lower path, on the other hand, consists of crumbling snow-covered platforms and ladders that collapse after being stepped on and reappear shortly after. Both paths eventually converge midway through the first section, leading into a short platforming sequence on icy platforms suspended above spike-filled pits.
Immediately afterward is a long segment involving crumbling snow platforms, where players must quickly advance along a collapsing trail to avoid falling into the spikes below. This is followed by a large vertical shaft filled with rising ice platforms. Players must descend carefully while avoiding being crushed or falling into the abyss, eventually reaching the door to the stage's miniboss room, decorated with a frozen Chill Penguin and housing the \hyperlink{miniboss:Eyezard}{Eyezard} miniboss. This enemy shields itself in ice and takes on different forms depending on its remaining health. It may charge players in claw form, bounce around the room to cause falling stalactites, or launch ice spikes itself when below half health. Its weak point is the red core, and unsurprisingly, Fire-type weapons are highly effective—melting its ice armor and exposing the vulnerable core.


The second half of the stage takes place within the Snow Base itself. Here, large ice blocks obstruct nearly every part of the path, forcing players to carve their way forward by destroying them. However, some blocks contains enemies or bombs, functioning as traps. Following, the last part of the stage focuses on \hyperlink{enem:Ice_Wings}{Ice Wings} enemies, that occasionally appear on-screen and, if not destroyed quickly, will freeze parts of the stage. This process makes platforms more slippery, creates damaging icicles, and encases other enemies in ice. Frozen enemies do not move or shoot, but they are also covered in spikes, which are launched outward if the enemy is destroyed or touched. Fortunately, the freezing effect is not instantaneous, and players have a brief window to intercept and destroy the Ice Wings before they complete their action.
Once this final stretch has been cleared, players reach the boss door, leading to their confrontation with Frost Walrus.

\subsection{Frost Walrus}\label{boss:Frost_Walrus}

Frost Walrus had always been something of a loose cannon—a rowdy, short-tempered Reploid with an impulsive urge for destruction. Despite this, General offered him a place among the Repliforce ranks, which Walrus gladly accepted, believing that a soldier’s duty was to destroy everything in sight~\cite{Xcoll1:Manual_X4}. Even so, his personality did not change, and he continued to cause trouble within Repliforce. At one point in his life, Walrus was even suspected of being a Maverick: according to~\cite{Xcoll1:Manual_X4}, he was under suspicion of Maverick behavior and was enlisted for disposal before joining Repliforce; alternatively,~\cite{wayback:X4_resources} suggests that General himself initiated investigations into Walrus’s behavior. Nevertheless, when General launched his coup, he needed every capable soldier and thus assigned Walrus to guard a secret weapon being developed at the North Pole. Walrus gladly accepted the task,  eager for battle and the opportunity to cause more destruction, welcoming war at open arms. This would be his final assignment, ending in his fatal confrontation with X and Zero, who ultimately put an end to his overconfidence and cocky attitude.

As a boss, Frost Walrus is one of the largest among the eight main Mavericks in the series, but despite his size, his attack repertoire is relatively limited. His primary attack, \emph{Tackle}, involves sliding across the arena and ramming into a wall, creating a shockwave that can knock players off the walls. When his health drops below two-thirds, he gains access to the \emph{Ice Blade} attack, in which he launches four icicles from his shoulder into the air; these fall and embed themselves into the ground, acting as hazards. Finally, when his health is below one-third, he uses the \emph{Frost Tower}, creating a massive icicle and then shattering it with a punch, scattering shards across the ground and walls.


Despite his imposing presence, the battle against Walrus is arguably the easiest among the eight Mavericks. His large size makes him an easy target for X’s shots, and during his slide, his lowered hitbox allows players to dash-jump over him. Combined with his relatively slow sliding speed, this makes it easy to avoid taking damage. Furthermore, all of his ice-based hazards can be neutralized—both icicles and shards are destroyed by a single charged shot from X (which also passes through them) or a single sword swing from Zero. Walrus is also extremely vulnerable to fire-based attacks, specifically Rising Fire and Ryuenjin, which melt his ice armor and force him into a predictable pattern of sliding followed by Frost Tower. While these weapons require close-range use—posing some risk for X—they are well-suited to Zero’s naturally close-combat style.

Upon defeat, X obtains the Frost Tower (\ref{Frost_Tower}), while Zero learns the Hyouretsuzan (\ref{Hyouretsuzan}). In the original Japanese version, his name is given as Frost Kibatodos (``Frost Steller Sea Lion'').

\section{Memorial Hall}
Once half of the eight main bosses have been defeated, Colonel will contact X or Zero and challenge them to a duel, forcing players to pick as next location the Memorial Hall. By itself, the Memorial Hall is a very simple stage, as it houses only a boss fight against Colonel (\ref{boss:Colonel}). Importantly, the boss fight is accessible to X and only to X, as when playing as Zero a cutscene will play in place of the actual fight.

\section{Space port}
Once all eight Mavericks have been defeated, players gain access to the first of the final three stages: the Space Port. In this mission, Maverick Hunters X and Zero are tasked with stopping the Repliforce, who are fleeing Earth to relocate to the heavily armed space colony, \emph{Final Weapon}.

The stage plays much like a traditional Mega Man level, though it is relatively short. It combines platforming and enemy busting, beginning with an exterior section of jumps and hazards, followed by an interior area packed with foes. The final stretch is a vertical climb leading to a showdown with Colonel, who has stayed behind to buy his forces time to escape.


\subsection{Colonel}\label{boss:Colonel}
\begin{figure}[htp]

Colonel was a young officer in Repliforce and the older brother of Iris. Created by Repliforce scientists as the perfect soldier and model officer, he was gifted with a strong will~\cite{wiki:Colonel} and great pride, showing compassion only toward his subordinates and his sister~\cite{Xcoll1:Manual_X4}. An exceptionally skilled swordsman, Colonel was said to rival even Zero’s abilities~\cite{wayback:X4_resources}, and the two shared a mutual respect that bordered on friendship.

As part of Repliforce, Colonel was among the first Reploids to respond to the disaster at Sky Lagoon, rushing in to rescue his sister. Luckily, he found out she had already been saved by Zero, thus developing a debt of pride toward the Hunter. However, when X and Zero encountered Colonel at the scene and demanded he disarm under suspicion of Maverick behavior, he refused—preferring to be labeled a Maverick rather than surrender his weapon and pride. As a result, Colonel and the entire Repliforce were declared Mavericks, prompting General to launch a coup and declare independence from humanity, igniting the Great Repliforce War. During the coup, Colonel remained largely in the background, except for one duel against X or Zero in the Repliforce Memorial Hall. In X’s case, Colonel fought to stop what he saw as the Hunters’ greatest threat to Repliforce, only to be defeated and forced to retreat. In Zero’s case, the battle was interrupted by Iris, who had fallen in love with Zero and reminded her brother of his debt to the Hunter. Once Repliforce completed its preparations and began evacuating to the Final Weapon space station, Colonel stayed behind to hold off X and Zero, buying his forces time to escape. This second confrontation proved fatal, leaving him mortally wounded. Even in his final moments, he held fast to the belief that Repliforce’s dream would be realized, asking Zero only to tell Iris that her brother died happily.

In battle, Colonel demonstrates his swordsmanship through a variety of sword-based techniques. His most basic move is the \emph{Shockwave}, in which he fires three energy slashes in a low–high–low pattern. He also uses \emph{Teleport}, briefly vanishing and reappearing next to the player for a sword slash, immediately followed by a retreating dash. These are the only two attacks he performs against X during the Memorial Hall encounter. Additionally, in his final confrontation, Colonel adds two more moves: the \emph{Thunder attack}, announced with the shout ``\textit{Energy Breaker}'', sends several energy bolts across the ground, which then rise skyward once evenly spaced, and the \emph{Giant Shockwave}, signaled by ``\textit{Ground Crush}'', sees Colonel leap high into the air and slam into the ground, releasing a forward-traveling shockwave that grows in size as it moves. This last move is only used when Colonel’s health drops below half.

At first glance, Colonel’s speed and high damage output make him seem like a difficult opponent. However, all of his attacks are predictable and can be countered with practice. The \emph{Shockwave} always follows the same low–high–low sequence, and players can evade it with a dash, a jump, and another dash in rhythm. The \emph{Teleport} is telegraphed by his brief disappearance, and its hitbox is smaller than it appears—a simple jump, or X’s hover for extra safety, avoids it entirely. Importantly, Colonel becomes intangible during his retreating dash, meaning he deals no contact damage but also cannot be harmed. Finally, Both the \emph{Thunder attack} and the \emph{Giant Shockwave} are verbally announced. The former can be dodged by jumping over the ground bolts and landing between them, while the latter requires only a wall jump to avoid. Offensively, Colonel often leaves himself open after attacking, especially during \emph{Shockwave} and \emph{Thunder Attack}, which have long recovery times. For extra damages, ice-based weapons—Frost Tower and Hyouretsuzan—are the most effective, though they require close range.

\section{Final Weapon 1}
The second-to-last stage of the game takes place in the first half of the so-called \textit{Final Weapon}, a massive space station intended to serve as the foundation for a Reploid-only nation in deep space. The station is also equipped with a colossal cannon aimed at Earth, meant as both a deterrent and a defensive weapon against human attacks. In-game, the station is divided into two stages: the first leads to the main chamber where Repliforce’s leader, General, resides, while the second takes players deeper into the station, eventually reaching its core.

The peculiarity of the Final Weapon 1 stage becomes clear immediately. After a short skirmish with a few enemies, players are confronted with a boss door right at the start. Uniquely, this stage puts players against \emph{two} major bosses: either Double or Iris (depending on the chosen character) at the very beginning, and General at the end.
The path between these encounters is also unusual, as players are offered two possible routes to the final boss door. The upper route emphasizes precision platforming, with spiked pits and walls designed to hinder wall jumps, as well as laser traps reminiscent of those in Storm Owl’s stage. The lower route instead focuses on combat and platforming over bottomless pits, though it provides a Ride Armor Eagle to ease part of the challenge. Both paths ultimately converge at the entrance to the final confrontation with General.

\subsection{Iris}\label{boss:Iris}

Iris was a reploid developed by Repliforce scientists as Colonel's younger sister, embodying a gentle soul who wished only for peace in contrast to Colonel's stern attitude. Although she originally worked for Repliforce, Iris was later reassigned as a Maverick Hunter navigator, assisting hunters from the base and acting as a liaison between the two forces~\cite{book:MH_field_guide}. During this period, she came into contact with Zero, who shared a mutual respect for Colonel. The three formed a bond, with Iris in particular developing feelings for Zero. However, those peaceful days were destined to end when Iris was kidnapped by Eregion and brought to Sky Lagoon, getting caught in the incident. Iris was gravely wounded, and it was only Zero’s intervention that saved her life, with Colonel arriving shortly afterward to check on his sister. Iris was therefore present when Colonel refused to disarm, rejecting cooperation with the Hunters and leading to Repliforce being branded as Mavericks. Despite her brother’s allegiance to Repliforce, Iris remained loyal to her role as navigator, supporting Zero in his missions. Yet, much like with her brother, Iris’s convictions soon began to clash with Zero’s; she begged him to avoid fighting, especially against Colonel, while Zero saw no other solution than to face his enemies head-on.

This conflict in Iris’s heart deepened after Colonel challenged Zero to a duel in the Memorial Hall, as Iris rushed in to stop them and managed to dissuade Colonel only by reminding him of the debt he owed to Zero, making him retreat. However, Iris’s words could not prevent a second clash: when Repliforce began escaping into space, Zero Zero headed to the Space Port convinced he had to stop Colonel and Repliforce, leaving Iris to watch as her brother was slain by the man she loved. Overcome with grief, she fled to the space colony ``Final Weapon,'' fully aligning herself with Repliforce’s goals. There, when Zero began his assault on the colony, Iris chose to intercept him and avenge her brother’s death. Lacking combat ability of her own~\cite{wayback:X4_resources}, she decided to install Colonel’s core crystal~\cite{wiki:Iris} (also referred to as his ``memory chip'' or simply his ``essence''~\cite{wayback:X4_resources,book:MH_field_guide}) into her body, thus fulfilling, although unwillingly, her creators’ original intention for her (see Chapter~\ref{char:Iris}). This act transformed her into the ``Ultimate Battle Reploid,'' granting her immense combat power and covering her body in massive armor. However, the incompatibility between her program and Colonel’s, born from their contradictory natures, caused severe rejection symptoms, and Iris lost control of her powers. Therefore, Zero was forced to fight her until her armor was destroyed, reverting her Iris normal state. Still, the injuries she sustained in the battle proved fatal, leaving her only a few moments of life. In her final moments, as she lay in Zero’s arms, Iris confessed her true wish: to live in a world inhabited only by reploids alongside Zero and her brother, even if it remained only a fantasy. As she died, Zero was left alone, questioning the purpose of his battles.

As the ``Ultimate Battle Reploid'', Iris appears clad in heavy, Ride Armor-like plating, which grants her the ability to fly and, most importantly, renders her completely immune to all damage. Despite her formidable power, however, Iris’s gentle heart is reflected in her behavior throughout the battle. She generally approaches the player at a slow pace, attempting to close the distance and inflict contact damage; only occasionally does she retreat to a corner to fire her \emph{Laser}, which sweeps across the floor. The only other way she can harm the player is through her innate counterattack system, which causes her to deploy two \emph{Floating Mines} whenever she is struck. These mines, much like Iris herself, move steadily toward Zero, albeit slightly faster, but they can be destroyed by any type of attack.

While avoiding damage from Iris is relatively straightforward, inflicting damage on her is the true challenge. As noted earlier, Iris is completely immune to all attacks, and striking her only pushes her back  while deploying additional mines. The sole way to harm her is to target Colonel’s Core, which emerges and floats alongside her only if forced out of her armor. The primary method requires players to land eight times before she fires her Laser (which resets the counter), causing to deploy sixteen mines, the maximum possible, in the process. A secondary condition can also reveal the core regardless of hits: if Zero’s health drops below 8 HP, Iris exposes the core herself, symbolizing her reluctance to truly harm him. Regardless of how it is revealed, however, attacking the core remains difficult, as it constantly floats around Iris and is hard to strike consistently, especially given Zero’s limited range. Matters are further complicated by the fact that the core’s primary weakness is the \emph{Ryuenjin}, which requires precision to land effectively. More importantly, while the core usually remains passive, it occasionally assists Iris by repositioning itself beneath the player and firing its own Laser a few seconds after hers. If players manage to avoid being caught in the crossfire, however, this creates an ideal opening to strike the core, as it is likely to be far away from Iris.

General was the supreme commander of Repliforce, a mighty leader respected by every soldier under his command. A massive reploid, he complemented his incredible strength with a strong will, a sense of justice and dignity, and a cultured knowledge, as symbolized by the bookshelves in his room during X's route introduction scene~\cite{wiki:General,wayback:X4_resources}. Although similar in ideals to his second-in-command, Colonel, General tended to use softer words when addressing his goals, particularly when speaking about his human creators. Interestingly, in the Japanese script for X4, General refers to himself using a pronoun typical of middle-aged men, emphasizing his role as a wise and seasoned veteran rather than a fresh soldier.

As leader of Repliforce, General’s task was to lead his troops in assisting the Maverick Hunters in their missions. However, as time went on, Repliforce’s efforts failed to meet expectations, leading the government’s high command to question whether Repliforce was useful at all, or even potentially dangerous. Amid this climate, General was approached by Sigma, who warned him of the danger the Hunters posed and suggested he strike first. General, however, ignored Sigma’s advice and simply dismissed him, claiming he had no intention of betraying humanity. Sigma left, laughing, as his true was merely to sow doubt in General’s mind. Shortly afterward, Sigma ordered Magma Dragoon to sabotage Sky Lagoon’s reactor, causing the city to crash and leaving Repliforce framed as the culprits. Wishing to protect his soldiers from the false accusation, General declared a coup d’état, intending to migrate Repliforce to space and establish a reploid-only nation free from human interference. On that occasion, however, General stressed that they did not aim to fight humanity, insisting that Repliforce was neither rebelling nor rising in insurrection against their creators, but only seeking freedom and security.


Following this decision, Repliforce began preparing to flee aboard the space station Final Weapon, though their efforts were hindered by the Maverick Hunters’ intervention, particularly by X and Zero, who ultimately attacked the station itself. There, they confronted General, accusing his actions to be cause of countless victims. Surprisingly, General did not back down, taking full responsibility and declaring that, branded as Mavericks, fighting was their only option and that the struggle for independence always required sacrifice~\cite{wordpress:X4_japanese_script_X,wordpress:X4_japanese_script_Z}. General then proceeded to face the Hunters himself, but despite his incredible size and power, he too succumbed to X and Zero’s assault, receiving severe damage to his body. However, he was spared destruction when Sigma hijacked Final Weapon’s main cannon and aimed it at Earth in an attempt to wipe out humanity, letting General realize he had been serving Sigma’s interests all along. Regaining clarity, he asked X and Zero to reach the weapon’s core and defeat Sigma and stop the wepon. X and Zero succeed, but even with Sigma gone, the cannon already reached the point of no return and got ready to fire. Knowing this catastrophe was his fault for falling into Sigma’s schemes, General took the only action left to him as a soldier: he placed what remained of his giant body in the cannon’s line of fire, absorbing the blast and destroying the weapon at the cost of his own life. In his final words, General asked forgiveness in the name of all Repliforce, while affirming that, as a soldier, he must follow his men who had died because of him.

\subsection{Phantom Sigma}
The true mastermind behind Repliforce's rebellion, Sigma presents himself as the game's final boss. Once the leader of the 17th Maverick Hunter Elite unit, Sigma was the perfect reploid—a special A-class hunter admired and relied upon by all, gifted with immense strength and reasoning power. This changed when he was called to deal with a dangerous red Maverick who had awakened and killed every reploid who drew near: Sigma confronted the threat to defend his soldiers, and challenged the out-of-control Zero to a duel, but his pride was shattered when Zero defeated and mocked him while laughing maniacally. Sigma survived only by chance, as Zero suddenly succumbed to a mental block, giving Sigma the opportunity to strike back and neutralize Zero. Alive by a miracle, Sigma ordered his soldiers to bring the mysterious reploid to Dr.~Cain for analysis, while he himself went under repair for the heavy damage sustained in the fight. Little did he know, however, that during the battle the Maverick Virus within Zero’s body had spread into him, slowly dragging him to the dark side. Several months later, the virus’s grip on Sigma was complete, twisting his vision of the world until he came to see humanity as an obstacle to reploid evolution~\cite{wayback:X4_resources}. Harnessing his genius and power, he began to subdue countless reploids and soldiers, rising in revolt against humanity, but was stopped by the combined efforts of X and Zero three times.

To that end, he planted seeds of doubt in General’s mind and framed Repliforce for the sabotage of Sky Lagoon, thereby igniting hostilities between the two factions, while he remained in the shadows, watching as Repliforce, X, Zero, Iris, and Double all played into his hands. In the end, Sigma decided to act directly, having assisted in first person at General's defeat, by hijacking Final Weapon’s main cannon—built by Repliforce as a deterrent against human attacks—and preparing to fire it at Earth to annihilate humanity, but is reached by X and Zero, in an attempt to stop him . Before the battle, he mocked X for his ingenuity that had allowed Double to spy on him so easily, and Zero for once being a Maverick, claiming that destruction had always been his true nature. Although Sigma’s body was destroyed once more, he left laughing, having already set the cannon to fire and delayed the Hunters long enough. It was only General’s sacrifice, placing his body in the cannon’s path, that prevented the catastrophe Sigma sought.

Following Phantom Sigma's defeat, his body explodes and opens a passage to Final Weapon's core, where Sigma attacks using multiple bodies at once, known as Gunner Sigma and Earth Sigma, accompanied by the Mini Body drones. Just like his previous form, these too follow a strict attack pattern, meaning players can always predict the next move: the Mini Bodies attack first, followed by Earth Sigma, then another strike from the Mini Bodies, and finally Gunner Sigma closing the sequence. Notably, each of the two main Sigma forms has its own separate health bar, so when one is defeated the other replace it in the rotation.


The Mini Bodies are three drones shaped like Sigma's head, each one colored according to its element: red for fire, blue for ice, and yellow for electricity. They represent the only random factor in the battle, as the attacking drone is chosen at random, forcing players to remain alert and adapt quickly. Only one drone attacks at a time, while the others act as physical obstacles or platforms, though none deal contact damage except when they deploy their spikes. When the red drone attacks, it appears on the far left while the other two stack to the right to form a wall, and uses the \emph{Flame Shot}, firing three sets of four fireballs each after aligning vertically with the player, making the best solution to avoid them moving rapidly up and down the makeshift wall. If the yellow drone appears in the center, with the others to the sides, it signals it will use the \emph{Electromagnetic Shot}, firing six fast electric spheres aimed directly at the player, which split upon impact into two bolts traveling horizontally. To avoid this, players should keep moving and use the drones as platforms. Finally, the blue drone mirrors the red, appearing on the far right but releasing an \emph{Ice Fog} across the floor. This is the easiest attack to avoid, as players must simply stay off the ground until the attack ends. Dealing with the Mini Bodies is essential for survival: they are Sigma’s most frequent attack, so mastering how to dodge them allows players some margin for error against the main bodies. It is important to note that the drones do not count as Sigma’s actual body, so destroying them serves only to interrupt the attack cycle, as they regenerate anyway.

Ultimately, this final Sigma battle is a test of reflexes and adaptability. While each attack is manageable on its own, the sheer combination of patterns, their frequency, the inability to permanently remove the Mini Bodies, and the difficulty of consistently striking Sigma’s small weak points can cause players to accumulate damage faster than they deal it, leading to defeat over time. Sub-Tanks provide relief, but players must remember they do not refill upon defeat, and the game’s pick-ups only restore them partially, thus requiring multiple attempts to fully recharge them. As such, they should be saved for moments when victory is within reach.
 
Upon defeating Sigma, a cutscene plays showing General’s sacrifice, immediately followed by an FMV unique to the character used, and then the game’s credits.

\chapter{Miscellaneous}\label{X4:misc} %
In this chapter we provide a series of less known fact and glitches related to the game.

\section{Saber Dash Cancel}\label{SDC}
The Saber Dash Cancel (or SDC for short) is a technique used by speedrunners to quickly defeat enemies and bosses when using Zero. The key to this technique lies in the fact that almost all enemies and bosses (except Cyber Peacock and Frost Walrus~\cite{wiki:X4_tricks}) do not trigger invincibility frames when hit by the first and second swings of Zero's sword. By combining this with the ability to cancel the sword swing animation with a dash—which can then be canceled by another sword swing—players can create a rapid sequence of hits that completely ignores invincibility frames. This sequence can quickly decimate bosses' health and is performed simply by rapidly alternating the attack and dash buttons. File \path{videos/X4/saber_dash_cancel} gives a visual example of how SDC can be performed.


There also exists a minor variation called the \emph{Double Saber Dash Cancel} (or SSDC)~\cite{rta:x4}, which consists of performing two slashes before dashing out.


\section{Extra Air Movement}
This minor exploit allows players to gain a second air dash, jump (if Zero has Kuuenbu), or Nova Strike (if X has the Ultimate Armor) while in the air. To perform it, players must simply get hit while airborne, which refreshes these techniques and allows them to use them again~\cite{wiki:X4_tricks}.


\section{Dash Speed out of Ryuenjin}
This trick is achieved by inputting the Ryuenjin command while also pressing the dash button. As a result, Zero performs the technique, but the speed he gains afterward matches the dash speed~\cite{wiki:X4_tricks}.


\section{Web Spider Invincibility Bug}\label{sec:spider_if_glitch}
This dangerous bug can occur during Web Spider's rematch. If players were to start the fight by immediately releasing a charged Twin Slasher as Web Spider climbs up, a bug triggers in which Spider’s health bar is completely emptied while he remains frozen in place, taking no damage from further attacks. This leads to an endless fight that only ends when the player is defeated~\cite{wiki:X4_tricks},as seen in file \path{videos/X4/Spider_softlock.mp4}.

\section{Web Spider Off-Screen Kill}
In contrast to the previous bug, this glitch allows players to deal massive damage to Web Spider, potentially killing him instantly and exploits the brief moment when Spider loses all invincibility while hiding in the trees~\cite{rta:x4}. To perform it, players must hit Spider and trigger his I-frames as he disappears, causing him to loose them  for the entire time he remains hidden. At this point, they should climb to the very top of the arena and attack ideally with the Plasma Shot, Nova Strike, or, in Zero's case, Kuuenbu. Since Spider is still present in the map, if set up correctly, he can take heavy damage, potentially skipping his second phase. Once the second phase begins, however, this glitch can no longer be performed. The video file \path{videos/X4/Spider_nova_glitch.mp4} shows this glitch in action.


\section{Split Mushroom Miniboss Skip}\label{x4_misc_mushroom_skip}
This skip can only be performed by X. By using either the Nova Strike and arm parts with Rising Fire, or the Lightning Web and foot parts, players can bypass the miniboss entirely. To do so, they must jump from the ledge just before the platform where the fight normally occurs and release a charged Rising Fire immediately followed by a Nova Strike. Alternatively, they can substitute Rising Fire with the Lightning Web and Nova Strike with a regular air dash, though this narrows the window of success. If executed correctly, X gains enough height to bypass the camera lock trigger while still triggering the miniboss, allowing him to skip the fight entirely. File \path{videos/X4/Mushroom_midboss_skip.mp4} shows how to perform this glitch using the first method.

\section{Starting Magma Dragoon's Fight Near Him}
This small trick is exclusive to X when equipped with the Body Parts and a charged Nova Strike. Players must slide down the wall leading to Dragoon's arena and perform a Nova Strike as X falls. If timed correctly, X lands directly next to Dragoon instead of at the usual position on the left. If the timing fails, however, X falls into the lava and dies as the fight begins. File \path{videos/X4/Dragoon_close_start.mp4} shows how to achieve such glitch.

\section{Magma Dragoon's Armor Parts Using the Ride Armor}\label{misc:armor_body}
This trick relies on two glitches involving the Ride Armor and allows X to use it to break the wall sealing the armor capsule instead of the usual charged Twin Slasher.  

The first glitch allows players to push an empty Ride Armor using Lightning Web, which pushes the armor depending on how the web expands, potentially even into walls. If pushed upward, the armor falls normally due to gravity, but if it becomes stuck inside a wall, it is considered ``on ground’’ and remains suspended.  
\begin{figure}[htp]
	\centering
	\includegraphics[height=3cm]{figures/X4/Magma_Dragoon/Dragoon_armor_glitch.png}
	\caption{Glitching the Ride Armor inside a wall}
\end{figure}

The second glitch allows the Ride Armor to glide through the air. The exact setup is unclear, but the most common method is placing the armor at the edge of a ledge, then performing a dash attack while getting hit at the same time. If successful, the armor is pushed off the ledge and begins gliding at dash speed, allowing players to reach otherwise inaccessible areas.  

By combining these glitches, players can push the armor near the end of the Volcano up to the highest part of the stage using the Lightning Web, then trigger the glide glitch by getting hit near the border of the upper platform using the \hyperref[enem:Giga_Death]{Giga Death} enemy. When executed correctly, the armor can glide onto the platform hiding the Body Parts and break the wall. File \path{videos/X4/Dragoon_capsule_with_armor.mp4} shows how to perform such glitch.


\section{Fighting Slash Beast Using the Ride Armor}
Using the same glitches, players can bring the Ride Armor into the Slash Beast fight: first, they must push the armor up onto the train engine, normally the obstacle preventing its advance; then, by baiting an enemy attack, they trigger the glide glitch to fly over the pits with the armor. Once on the last train, they push the armor forward so that when the train departs, both the player and the armor ride together. If the setup fails, the armor is left behind, if successful, players can enter Slash Beast’s arena with the Ride Armor. File \path{videos/X4/Beast_armor_glitch.mp4} and \path{videos/X4/Beast_vs_Armor.mp4} gives visuals clues on how to perform the glitch and its effect.

\section{Storm Owl's ``Shield Break''}
During Storm Owl's fight, there are instances where the boss loses all invincibility frames, allowing players to deal heavy damage with multi-hit attacks~\cite{rta:x4}.  

The first method involves baiting his four-shot cyclone attack, waiting for him to fire the last shot, and striking just before he makes them converge. When Owl spreads his arms to recall the spheres, his invincibility is removed and will not activate for the entire duration of the animation (about 59 frames), allowing spheres from plasma shots to strike consecutively.  

The second method involves hitting Owl with his weakness, the Aiming Laser. Upon impact, he enters a short stun before retreating, during which he also loses any invincibility associated with the Nova Strike. Depending on the retreat direction, this attack can deal up to 80\% of his health in one strike~\cite{rta:x4}.

\section[Storm Owl's Weakness Glitch]{Storm Owl's Weakness Glitch (Legacy Collection Only)}
This glitch, exclusive to the Legacy Collection release, occurs if the rotating cyclone attack is interrupted by hitting Storm Owl with the Aiming Laser. When this happens, the attack is abruptly canceled, causing him to fly off-screen, but when he next uses the same attack, he reappears already performing it, with the cyclone stream rotating as he moves toward the arena’s center, where a new set of cyclones is generated~\cite{wiki:Storm_owl}. This latter part can be seen in file \path{videos/X4/Owl_glitched_cross.mp4}.

\section{Exiting Storm Owl Boss Room}
This glitch allows players to escape the boss arena and enter a glitched zone with Owl following them. Performing it requires near frame-perfect timing, using the Lightning Web to wall-jump in the air and skip the trigger that locks the camera. Because of the precision required, this glitch has only been documented in TAS~\cite{Owl_glitch_TAS}.


\section{Frost Walrus Glitched Area}
It is possible to enter a glitched area in Frost Walrus’s stage, though it contains nothing of value. Players must first reach the miniboss room entrance, then backtrack and climb up to the EX-Tank location. Due to the boss room loading, the camera shifts right, allowing them to continue along the platform. By descending from the right, they can enter the glitched area, but it offers no advantages or skips.

\section{Multiple Hits on Colonel}
Every time Colonel’s animation changes, his invincibility frames reset. As a result, players can hit him multiple times with a single attack. This glitch applies to both X and Zero.

\section{Fighting General Using a Ride Armor}
A glitch similar to the one used to face Slash Beast with the Ride Armor can also be performed against General. However, it requires several frame-perfect inputs, making it much more difficult. If executed, players can attack General with the Ride Armor, dealing massive damage. So far, this glitch has only been performed during TAS~\cite{General_TAS}, with no known recordings on original hardware.


\section{Ultimate Armor and Black Zero}
At the start of a new game, players can enter two secret passwords, one for each playable character. For X, the password requires selecting him and inputting the sequence $\circ\circ\leftarrow\leftarrow\leftarrow\leftarrow\leftarrow\leftarrow$ (six left inputs), then holding L1 and R1 before pressing Start. If successful, X’s color palette becomes slightly darker, with light purple replacing the light blue under his armor. From then on, entering any armor capsule grants him the Ultimate Armor instead of the regular parts.  

For Zero, the code requires highlighting him on the character selection screen, holding R1, and inputting $\rightarrow\rightarrow\rightarrow\rightarrow\rightarrow\rightarrow$ (six right inputs), then releasing R1, holding B, and pressing Start. If entered correctly, Zero appears in his black armor. This change, however, is purely cosmetic and does not alter gameplay.
