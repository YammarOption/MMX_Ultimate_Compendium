\chapter{Game overview}
\textit{Mega Man X4} is the fourth installment in the Mega Man X series. Initially intended to be a Sega Saturn exclusive, the game was ultimately released on both the Sega Saturn and PlayStation in August and September 1997 in Japan and America, respectively. This made it the second game in the franchise to be available on these platforms, following its predecessor. The European version, however, was released exclusively for the PlayStation in October 1997. The game later became available for PC in Japan in December of the following year and in America and Europe in June 1999.

While retaining the series' core features, \textit{Mega Man X4} significantly changes its visual style, transitioning from a 16-bit to a fully 32-bit art style. Character sprites were entirely redesigned, nearly doubling the total number of animations~\cite{wikip}, and the quality of the backgrounds was also enhanced. Due to the large number of sprites, the developers implemented a compression routine to fit everything, which resulted in almost all stages being split into two sections. Additionally, this is the first game in the series to feature fully voiced FMV cutscenes, although its predecessor had non-voiced ones. Notably, the game also marks the first time that Zero is fully playable, offering a radically different playstyle and a dedicated campaign compared to X.

Overall, the game was praised for the changes it introduced to the series, particularly the addition of Zero and the updated graphics. However, it faced criticism for its limited innovation in gameplay compared to its predecessor and, most notably, for the poor quality of the voice acting, which is still remembered today. Interestingly, not all the game's elements received adaptation, as the eight bosses' battle quote kept their japanese voice acting (this does not apply to later bosses) and their pre-battle dialogue with the player characters were completely removed.

\section{Main plot}

In the year 21XX, the security of humans and reploids against Maverick attacks is ensured through the combined efforts of the Maverick Hunters and the Reploid Force, also known as \textit{Repliforce}. Like the Maverick Hunters, Repliforce is a military organization composed of reploids, with dedicated soldiers and scientists working to counter Maverick threats. However, unlike the Maverick Hunters, Repliforce follows a strict, military-based structure, consisting of soldiers ranked up to General, its supreme commander, and organized similarly to a traditional army, divided into forces such as land, air, and marine, all with the task of dealing with large-scale emergencies. However, following the failure of Sigma's leadership and Doppler's vaccine to stop the mavericks, Dr.~Cain decides to reorganize Repliforce into a V3 version. This new structure aims to better support the Maverick Hunters in combating mavericks by reducing response times to attacks, improving scientific collaboration between the Maverick Hunters and Repliforce scientists, and maximizing reploid efficiency~\cite{manual:X4}. However, six months after the reorganization, an evaluation shows that the operation has not been successful. The response times of the Maverick Hunters have not improved, and maverick attacks have increased at an alarming rate. As a result, Repliforce V3 is deemed ineffective and, also, potentially dangerous. Consequently, Cain decides to restructure the Maverick Hunters into a new V4 version, merging the 17th and 0th Units, led by X and Zero, respectively. This V4 reorganization proves successful, as the newly formed unit effectively responds to maverick attacks in the same capacity as Repliforce.

This is the context in which Mega Man X4 begins. The story opens with a revived and disguised Sigma approaching General, Repliforce's supreme commander, and attempting to persuade him. Sigma warns General about the danger the Maverick Hunters pose to all reploids, claiming that any reploid who defies humans is labeled as a maverick and therefore hunted. Sigma urges General to preemptively strike, asserting that it’s only a matter of time before the Hunters come for Repliforce. However, despite Sigma’s attempts, General dismisses his warnings and Sigma leaves, although with a smile, having sown doubt in General’s mind. Next, Sigma proceeds with his plan.l First, he manipulates Magma Dragoon, a Maverick Hunter, by exploiting his obsession with facing X in combat. Under Sigma's order, Dragoon leads an attack on the flying city of Sky Lagoon using a giant mechaniloid, inciting chaos and drawing Repliforce into battle~\cite{wiki:repliforce_war}. Maverick Hunters X and Zero are also dispatched, unaware of Repliforce’s presence, but when they arrive, Dragoon sabotages the city's flight systems, causing Sky Lagoon to crash with catastrophic casualties. X and Zero navigate the wreckage to eliminate the mechaniloid, with Zero rescuing Iris in the process. After the battle, Colonel,Repliforce’s second-in-command and Iris’s brother, arrives to protect her. Suspicious of Repliforce’s involvement, X and Zero ask Colonel to disarm and come with them, suspecting him of being a maverick, but Colonel refuses due to his pride as a soldier, even if it means being branded a Maverick. Consequently, Repliforce is blamed for Sky Lagoon's destruction, and its members are labeled Mavericks. Following these accusations, General declares Repliforce’s independence from humans, launching a coup to create a reploid-only nation for reploids’ freedom and safety.

X and Zero subsequently set out to stop Repliforce’s rebellion, X being supported by rookie-hunter Double, while Iris provide support to Zero. The pair manage to stop various mavericks attacks, and each one even face Colonel in a duel. X ultimately defeats Colonel, while Iris intervenes to prevent the confrontation between Zero and her brother, reminding Colonel of his debt to Zero. Once the primary Maverick threats are neutralized, X and Zero learn that Repliforce’s ship is leaving Earth for space, where a colony awaits them. X and Zero therefore rush to the spaceport, where Colonel stays behind to delay the Hunters and buy his soldiers more time to escape, but after a fierce battle, is defeated and ultimately dies. From here X continues to pursue Repliforce, while Zero briefly returns to base, only to find that Iris has left to join Repliforce, devastated by her brother’s death. At the same time, Double reveals himself as Sigma’s spy, killing two Hunters before pursuing X into the Final Weapon, Repliforce’s space colony and supposed utopia.

Upon reaching their destination the couple receive a bitter welcome, as X encounters Double who starts mocking him for his trust in others, while Zero finds Iris who, overcome with grief for her brother's death and decided to fulfill Colonel's wish, merges with Colonel’s core and attacks Zero. Both X and Zero succeed in defeating their opponents, though Zero remains heartbroken, as the damage he inflicted on Iris proves to be fatal. In her final moments, Iris expresses her dream of a world where she, Zero, and her brother could have lived peacefully and happily, leaving Zero questioning the purpose of his battles. Despite their sorrow, X and Zero press on, ultimately confronting and defeating General in hopes of stopping the coup. However, even with General defeated, the Final Weapon begins to move independently, aiming his arsenal toward Earth and revealing the presence of a hidden operator. This figure reveals himself to be Sigma, who faces off with the Maverick Hunters once more. After a fierce battle the couple emerge victorious, but despite Sigma's defeated, the weapon’s firing process remains active, leaving X and Zero uncertain of their next move. in this final moment General reappears, seeking forgiveness for Repliforce’s foolishness. General decides to sacrifice himself, using his damaged body to intercept the weapon’s blast and disabling it permanently. As the weapon collapses and explodes, X and Zero escape, each one carrying their own burdens: X questions the nature of a ``Maverick'' and makes Zero promise to end him if he ever becomes one; Zero, haunted by Iris and Sigma’s revelations, wonders about his true identity.


\section{Main Characters}

\subsection{X}
One of the main protagonist of the game, X (more detail in chapter~\ref{char:X}) He is the leader of the Maverick Hunters' 17th Elite Unit and a well-renowned Maverick Hunter, especially since he was responsible having defeated Sigma thrice in the past. X takes very seriously his task of protecting humans and reploids, but this clashes with his pacifist heart and benevolent spirit which refuses violence. This cause an inner conflict and a trouble very few people can understand.

X's origins are considered a mystery, as nobody knows why he was built or buried away~\cite{X4:Manual}.
\subsection{Zero}
The second protagonist of the game, Zero is an incredibly skilled SA-class maverick Hunter, leader of the special 0th unit and X's best friend. Unlikely X, Zero has a cold attitude, never hesitating to carry on his duty, and this makes him the perfect partner for X, especially since Zero is one of the very few people who seems to understand X's struggle~\cite{Xcoll1:Manual_X4}.

Just like X, Zero's origins are surrounded by mystery.

\subsection{Repliforce}

A military organization which works in parallel with the Maverick Hunters to deal with mavericks threat, with a particular focus on large-scale emergencies. At some point in time, Dr.~Cain reorganizes Repliforce's structure in a new ``V3'' program to improve collaboration between them and Maverick Hunters, and in hope to compensate the failure that were Sigma and Doppler. However, such upgrade did not provided the desired results, and Repliforce was pointed as ineffective and potentially dangerous. 

In the events of Mega Man X4, Repliforce is framed responsible for the disaster in Sky Lagoon, having been lured in by Magma Dragoon's attack and refusing to disarm when Maverick Hunters requested to  follow for interrogations. Due to this accusations, Repliforce's supreme commander, General, declare Repliforce independence and start a coup aimed to create a reploid-only nation in space.

\section{Game Mechanics}

Following the path set by its predecessors, Mega Man X4 continues to innovate its gameplay by introducing new features and modifying existing ones to keep the gameplay new yet familiar to long-time fans of the series. The main features in Mega Man X4 are as follows:
\begin{itemize}
	\item \textbf{Fully Playable Zero}: As an improvement over X3’s ``Zero Change'' feature, Mega Man X4 offers a fully playable Zero. Zero has his own dedicated campaign mirroring X’s own, and possesses his own \textit{learning system}, mimicking X's variable weapons system, which allow to obtain unique techniques from defeated bosses and collectible items. However, Zero cannot acquire any armor parts, as these are exclusive to X.
	\item \textbf{Sub Tank System Change}: Before Mega Man X4, collectible sub tanks consisted of four hidden sub tanks across the stages that could be filled by picking up energy drops from enemies on a one-to-one ratio, as long as the player’s health was full. Mega Man X4 modifies this system by reducing the total number of sub tanks to two and allowing them to be recharged each time a health pickup is collected, regardless of the player’s health. However, they are refilled by a fixed amount rather than the amount the player’s health would have restored~\cite{wiki:sub-tank}. Additionally, X4 compensates for the loss of two sub tanks by introducing two new types of sub tanks: a weapon sub tank, which functions similarly to the life tank, and the EX-item, which increases the starting lives from two to four when beginning a stage.
	\item The pink armor capsule system from the previous game has been removed.
	\item Similarly, the Ride Armor system has been changed back to the way it was in the X1-X2 games, with the removal of summoning platforms and upgrade modules.
	\item \textbf{Ride Chaser Return}: The Ride Chaser vehicles from Mega Man X2 make a return. However, unlike in X2 where they functioned more like Ride Armors, in this game, the Ride Chaser is featured in a high-speed, auto-scrolling section limited to a single stage.
	\item \textbf{Dual Fire Button}: Thanks to upgraded hardware, the game allows players to map two buttons to the fire command, one for the regular X-Buster and another for the sub-weapon. This enables players to use both the regular fire and sub-weapon at the same time, unlike previous games where only one could be used at once. However, it’s not possible to charge one while using the other, as firing a sub-weapon interrupts the charge of the main weapon and vice-versa.
	\item \textbf{Two-part stages}: A new element for this game consists in having most of the stages divided into two big areas, often differentiated by enemies inside and background/environment. Reaching the second area of a stage also act as an intermediate check point, as players have to choice to restart from the beginning of it should they receive a game-over.
\end{itemize}



\section{X's Weaponry}\label{X4:sub_weapon_X}

\subsection{\includegraphics[width=10px, height=10px]{figures/X4/weapons/Ico_web.png} Lightning Web}\label{Lightning_Web}
Obtained after defeating Web Spider~\ref{boss:Web_spider}, the Lightning Web fires a spider net charged with electricity that can be used to attack, wall jump, or double jump. Upon firing, X will shoot an electric projectile that travels a fixed distance before opening up. In this state, the web behaves like a wall for X to jump and wall-jump on, lasting for a short amount of time. Once X jumps onto it, the web disappears shortly after, regardless of the residual time. Additionally, this weapon deals damage only in its projectile form.

Upon charging, X fires a lightning web that subsequently opens up to eight more, covering a large portion of the screen and dealing continuous damage over time. This web, however, is non-solid and cannot be climbed on.


\subsection{\includegraphics[width=10px, height=10px]{figures/X4/weapons/Ico_frost.png} Frost Tower}\label{Frost_Tower}
When using the Frost Tower, X generates a large ice block that acts as a shield, which can also trap enemies before dissipating. Upon activation, X creates an ice block around him that remains stationary on the ground, serving as both an obstacle for enemies and a shield for X. Once activated, X can move freely since the ice block is not a solid obstacle for him, meaning he can hide behind it while shooting regularly with the X-Buster. Enemies, however, take contact damage from the ice, which lasts for a short time before shattering. Enemy projectiles, however, are not stopped by it. 

When used in the air, X briefly stops mid-air to create the ice block, which will promptly fall to the ground. Only one ice block can be present on the screen at a time; creating a second one causes the previous block to disappear.

When charged, this weapon causes various giant ice shards to fall from the sky in four waves, with an increasing number of falling shards: one, two, three, and four, for a total of ten shards. As with the regular version, X can move freely once the first wave begins, and the shards follow him as he moves.

This weapon is obtained only after X has defeated Frost Walrus~\ref{boss:Frost_walrus}.



\subsection{\includegraphics[width=10px, height=10px]{figures/X4/weapons/Ico_soul.png} Soul Body}\label{Soul_Body}
Soul Body is the weapon X obtains after defeating Split Mushroom~\ref{boss:Split_mushroom}. This weapon accumulates energy to generate an illusion, and enemies will sustain damage if struck by the soul energy. When activated, X creates a hologram of his silhouette in front of him that mimics his movements, remaining at a fixed distance and always positioned in front of X. If X turns, the copy will automatically reposition in front of him at the same distance. The hologram deals contact damage to enemies and dissipates after a certain amount of time or if it receives enough damage. Only one clone can be generated at a time.

When charged, X creates an unarmored clone of himself that the player can control. This clone can move only within the current screen and can fire using the regular, uncharged X-Buster. Moreover, it is immune to all damage and disappears only after a short period of time or if it falls into a pit. While controlling the clone, X remains frozen in the location where he created it (even if in mid-air), but he is immune to all damage.


\subsection{\includegraphics[width=10px, height=10px]{figures/X4/weapons/Ico_Rising.png} Rising Fire}\label{Rising_Fire}
When equipping the Rising Fire, X shoots a fireball straight upward, with unlimited vertical range but minimal horizontal reach. When fired in the air, X briefly stops mid-air, extending his airborne time. This weapon also has a fast firing rate, allowing X to shoot it multiple times in quick succession. 

When charged, Rising Fire causes X to rise into the air, performing a flaming uppercut in a Shoryuken-like manner, ending with a fireball release similar to the regular version. Performing the charged version in mid-air allows X to perform a second jump, increasing his vertical reach.

Rising Fire is the weapon X obtains after defeating Magma Dragoon~\ref{boss:Magma_dragoon}.

\subsection{\includegraphics[width=10px, height=10px]{figures/X4/weapons/Ico_hunter.png} Ground Hunter}\label{Ground_Hunter}
Acquired after defeating Jet Stingray~\ref{boss:Jet_stingray}, the Ground Hunter fires mini stingray drones that race across the ground, making it particularly effective against enemies on walls and floors. When firing normally, X can deploy up to three drones that glide down from the buster to the ground, then proceed to move forward, following the ground and even descending along walls. By pressing down while the stingray drones are in flight, players can command them to make an immediate 90-degree turn downward. This command applies to all drones on screen regardless of when they were fired, and firing with the down direction already pressed causes the drones to launch vertically.

When charged, X fires a single, larger drone that travels straight forward. Players can press up or down on the gamepad to release additional energy drones above and below as the main drone moves forward.

\subsection{\includegraphics[width=10px, height=10px]{figures/X4/weapons/Ico_aiming.png} Aiming Laser}\label{Aiming_Laser}

Defeating Cyber Peacock~\ref{boss:Cyber_peacock} grants X the access to the Aiming Laser. Equipping this weapon causes a cursor to appear on screen in front of X, which can be controller by players by pressing up or down on the gamepad to move it along its path. When the cursor moves over an enemy, it locks it and allows X to fire a laser aimed directly to that enemy, regardless of its position. Moreover, the cursor can lock up to three enemies at the time, and three laser of different colors (green,yellow and red) will come out at the same time aiming at those enemies without any additional costs Charging up the weapon will cause a fan-shaped laser to come out from the X buster for a short amount of time and that can be controlled just like the cursor in the regular version, dealing contact damage to all enemies that enter its range.


Defeating Cyber Peacock~\ref{boss:Cyber_peacock} grants X access to the Aiming Laser. Equipping this weapon causes a cursor to appear on the screen in front of X, which players can control by pressing up or down on the gamepad to move it along its path. When the cursor moves over an enemy, it locks onto that enemy, allowing X to fire a laser aimed directly at it, regardless of position. The cursor can lock onto up to three enemies at a time, and three lasers of different colors (green, yellow, and red) will simultaneously fire at the locked targets without additional energy cost.

When charged, the weapon causes a fan-shaped laser to emit from the X-Buster for a short duration. This charged laser can be controlled similarly to the cursor in the regular version, dealing contact damage to all enemies within its range.

\subsection{\includegraphics[width=10px, height=10px]{figures/X4/weapons/Ico_cyclone.png} Double Cyclone}\label{Double_Cyclone}

The Double Cyclone fires vacuum cyclones to the left and right, each curving upward in its respective direction before disappearing after a short time. If one of the projectiles hits an enemy, it will briefly stop in place before disappearing. Similar to the Rising Fire, firing this weapon in the air causes X to stall briefly. 

When charged, this weapon causes X to fire two large tornadoes to his left and right, covering a significant horizontal portion of the screen.

This weapon is obtained by X only after defeating Storm Owl~\ref{boss:Storm_owl}.

\subsection{\includegraphics[width=10px, height=10px]{figures/X4/weapons/Ico_slasher.png} Twin Slasher}\label{Twin_Slasher}

Defeating Slash Beast~\ref{boss:Slash_beast} grants X the Twin Slasher. When using this weapon, X shoots two crescent-shaped beams diagonally, which travel a certain distance, gaining size as they move forward. The energy blades move quickly and can pass through walls and ground, although only two pairs can be on screen at once, and each blade deals limited damage. However, the weapon's low cost and rapid fire rate compensate for its limited damage output.

When charged, the weapon causes X to fire a volley of blades—eight in total, split into two groups of four, providing increased range and damage.

\section{Zero's Techniques}\label{X4:sub_weapon_Z}

\subsection{\includegraphics[width=10px, height=10px]{figures/X4/weapons/Zero_1.png} Rakuhouha}\label{Rakuhouha}

Defeating Cyber Peacock~\ref{boss:Cyber_peacock} grants Zero access to Rakuhouha, a powerful attack that channels all stored energy into a single blast. When pressing the Giga Attack button while grounded, Zero punches the ground and shouts ``Get Ready'' before releasing nine bolts of energy that disperse in a fan shape, destroying most enemies in a single hit and even passing through walls.

This weapon is the only one that consumes Zero's energy meter. Due to its high damage output and range, it is also one of the most costly weapons in the game, depleting the energy meter in only four uses. However, this weapon restores some energy each time Zero is hit~\cite{wiki:Rakuhouha}.

\subsection{\includegraphics[width=10px, height=10px]{figures/X4/weapons/Zero_2.png} Kuuenbu/Kuuenzan}\label{Kuuenbu}

In a twist never seen before in the series, defeating Split Mushroom~\ref{boss:Split_mushroom} grants Zero not one, but two techniques, though they share the same slot in the weapon menu.

The first technique Zero obtains is Kuuenbu, which can essentially be considered a double jump. By pressing the jump button again while in the air, Zero performs a second jump, allowing him to reach higher in the air. However, this move cannot be performed after a dash jump or out of a Hienkyaku.

The second technique Zero obtains alongside Kuuenbu is Kuuenzan, a spinning blade attack performed by pressing the attack button while in the air. This technique permanently replaces the midair slash Zero starts with, and unlike the original midair slash, players can change the direction Zero is facing while performing the attack. This allows for slight movements left or right, enabling hit-and-run tactics.

\subsection{\includegraphics[width=10px, height=10px]{figures/X4/weapons/Zero_3.png} Raijingeki}\label{Raijingeki}
Raijingeki is the technique Zero obtains after defeating Web Spider~\ref{boss:Web_spider}. Activated by pressing the special-weapon button, this technique causes Zero to perform a forward thrust with his blade, infusing it with electric power. The charging process takes a short amount of time to complete, creating a delay between the input and the move’s execution, which leaves Zero vulnerable to attacks. However, the attack is immediate if used mid-combo, i.e., after the first or second slash of Zero's regular attack.

\subsection{\includegraphics[width=10px, height=10px]{figures/X4/weapons/Zero_4.png} Hienkyaku}\label{Hienkyaku}
Defeating Jet Stingray~\ref{boss:Jet_stingray} allows Zero to learn Hienkyaku. This techniques allows Zero to perform an air dash, by pressing the dash command while Zero is in the air. However, Zero cannot use this technique out of a dash-jump.

\subsection{\includegraphics[width=10px, height=10px]{figures/X4/weapons/Zero_5.png} Ryuenjin}\label{Ryuenjin}
Ryuenjin is the special technique Zero learns after defeating Magma Dragoon~\ref{boss:Magma_dragoon}. This technique, activated by pressing the up direction + special-weapon button, causes Zero to perform a powerful upward attack that burns enemies with a blade engulfed in flames, making Zero rise diagonally into the air. The attack deals damage for its entire duration, meaning a well-placed strike can destroy even the toughest enemies in one blow.

\subsection{\includegraphics[width=10px, height=10px]{figures/X4/weapons/Zero_6.png} Tenkuuha}\label{Tenkuuha}

Upon defeating Storm Owl~\ref{boss:Storm_owl}, Zero gains access to the Tenkuuha. This permanent upgrade allows the Z-Saber to destroy energy projectiles aimed at Zero with a single attack, while also changing its color to purple.


\subsection{\includegraphics[width=10px, height=10px]{figures/X4/weapons/Zero_7.png} Hyouretsuzan}\label{Hyouretsuzan}

When using Hyouretsuzan, Zero performs a downward thrust with his saber, covered in ice, by pressing down + the special-weapon button while in the air. This technique inflicts continuous damage while active, allowing Zero to deal heavy damage to most non-boss enemies. The technique ends when Zero reaches the ground, causing the ice to shatter, or if he performs a double jump out of it. In the latter case, Zero can immediately perform the move again. It is also possible to use this technique as a follow-up to Ryuenjin.

To obtain this technique, Zero must first defeat Frost Walrus~\ref{Frost_walrus}.


\subsection{\includegraphics[width=10px, height=10px]{figures/X4/weapons/Zero_8.png} Shippuuga}\label{Shippuuga}
Shippuuga is Zero's final technique, obtained after defeating Slash Beast~\ref{boss:Slash_beast}. Activated by pressing the special-weapon button while dashing, this attack causes Zero to perform a half-circular slash with his saber. This technique can only be executed while dashing on the ground.


\section{Fourth Armor}\label{X4:Armor}
Following the tradition established by previous games, Mega Man X4 introduces a new set of armor parts split into four different capsules hidden across distinct stages. However, unlike previous entries, Mega Man X4 breaks the tradition of having a secret fifth capsule obtainable by collecting all hidden collectibles. Obviously, the capsule system is exclusive to X's campaign, as armor capsules do not appear at all during Zero's campaign.

The Fourth Armor, also referred to in different sources as the \textit{Normal Armor}~\cite{book:Compendium} or \textit{Force Armor}~\cite{X5:manual}, consists of the following parts:

\begin{itemize}
	\item \emph{Foot Parts:} These allow X to perform an air dash, similar to previous games. Additionally, by pressing the jump button a second time while in the air, X can hover in place for a few seconds. While hovering, X can move slightly forward or backward without turning, though moving significantly reduces hover time. The capsule containing the Foot Parts is hidden in Web Spider's stage, in a side path behind a waterfall near the beginning.
	
	\item \emph{Body Parts:} Found in Magma Dragoon's stage, near the end of the second section and where the Ride Armor can be found, hidden behind a wall breakable only by using a charged Twin Slasher. These parts halve all incoming damage and convert it into energy for the Nova Strike—an invincible, high-damage air dash executed by pressing the Giga-Attack button.
	
	\item \emph{Arm Parts:} Upgrade the X-Buster, enabling the charge-up of special weapons and unlocking a third level of charged shots. Hidden in Storm Owl's stage, these parts are located in a secret path accessible only by using the Lightning Web before the stage's mini-boss. Uniquely, players will find two capsules and can choose one of two versions of this upgrade, which can be swapped any time by revisiting the other capsule:
	\begin{itemize}
		\item \emph{Stock Charged Shot:} Allows X to store up to four smaller charged shots, which are displayed as icons under X's health bar. Pressing the shoot button fires a stored charged shot instead of a regular one if available. The Arm Parts for this version are white and blue.
		\item \emph{Plasma Charged Shot:} Fires a single, larger charged shot that travels through enemies and walls, leaving up to three smaller plasma balls upon impact. These plasma balls deal continuous damage but take priority over other damage sources, canceling out other attacks. The Arm Parts for this version are black and yellow.
	\end{itemize}
	
	\item \emph{Head Parts:} Located in Cyber Peacock's stage, these are awarded for achieving an S Rank in the third time trial. These parts reduce the energy cost of all sub-weapons to zero for their regular fire, making them effectively infinite unless energy is used for charged attacks.
\end{itemize}

Although, as stated, there is no secret capsule in this game, players can unlock an alternate armor by inputting a special code on the character selection screen. If entered correctly, X appears with a darker color scheme and receives the full \emph{Ultimate Armor} set upon entering any capsule. This armor, which becomes a staple in subsequent games, replaces the secret capsule mechanic from previous entries.%A better description on who and how the armor was developed are instead given in section \ref{boss:X}

Technically, the Ultimate Armor functions identically to the regular Fourth Armor in most respects, except for its darker color scheme and the key addition of an infinite charge for the Nova Strike. This enhanced Nova Strike also features a larger hitbox, making it exceptionally effective for quickly defeating bosses.



\chapter{Stages}

\section{Opening Stage: Sky Lagoon}\label{X4:intro_stage}
Sky Lagoon is the first stage players encounter in the game, regardless of whether they choose X or Zero. The stage is split into two distinct halves: the first takes place in the floating city of Sky Lagoon, which is under attack by Maverick forces, while the second occurs in the ruins after the city's catastrophic fall. As with any introductory stage, this level serves as a tutorial to teach players the basic game mechanics.

The first area is a linear section where the stage boss, Eregion, intermittently attacks from the background or foreground, attacking with either its claws or by firing plasma shots. Players must progress while evading these attacks and dealing with shut doors, which can either be destroyed or jumped over, except for the final door, which has be destroyed. Destroying the final door causes Eregion to flee. Interestingly, players can also defeat Eregion by attacking its claws when it strikes, forcing it to retreat earlier without any significant consequences. After Eregion retreats, players traverse a long corridor filled with enemies, eventually reaching the generator room of Sky Lagoon. Here, X or Zero encounters Magma Dragoon, who declares that Sky Lagoon is doomed to fall before escaping. X or Zero are forced to teleport away as the floating city crashes to the ground.

The ruins of Sky Lagoon provide the setting for the second half of the stage. The primary threats here include falling debris, which can crush X or Zero, and \hyperlink{miniboss:trap_blast}{Trap Blast} enemies, which emerge from the debris. Near the end of the stage, the floor collapses, leading to a pit containing collectibles that requires players to wall-jump to escape. However, skilled players who move quickly can avoid falling into the pit altogether, reaching the next section where Eregion, the stage boss, awaits.


Sky Lagoon is home to following enemies:

\end{itemize}
\subsection{Eregion}\label{boss:Eregion}
Eregion is a giant, dragon-like Mechaniloid equipped with massive wings that allow it to soar through the skies and attack with electric shots fired from its mouth. The origins and purpose of Eregion remain unknown, the only certain thing being that under the orders of Magma Dragoon, Eregion wreaked havoc on the city of Sky Lagoon to serve as a distraction, giving Magma Dragoon the opportunity to sabotage the city's systems and cause its catastrophic crash to the ground. Surviving the impact due to its ability to fly, Eregion resumed its rampage, only to be ultimately stopped by X or Zero (with Zero arriving to rescue Iris).

As the first boss in the game, Eregion is relatively straightforward and manageable. During its initial encounter in Sky Lagoon, it attacks the player either by slamming his claw onto the ground, throwing a punch from below, piercing the floor, or firing plasma shots after flying in the foreground.
Players can retaliate during the Claw Slam and the punch attacks by striking its claws, dealing damage and eventually forcing it to retreat. However, damaging Eregion during this phase has no bearing on the final boss fight.

The actual battle against Eregion occurs at the end of the stage, within the ruins of Sky Lagoon. During this encounter, Eregion lands to engage the player directly and employs three main attacks~\cite{book:Compendium}: \emph{Claw Attack}, a wide swing of its claw aimed at the player; \emph{Tackle}, a flying swoop across the screen to reposition; \emph{Electromagnetic Shot}, an electric attacks from its mouth, either as a three-sphere projectile or a rapid stream that sweeps the ground. The variation depends on Eregion's remaining health.


As the first boss, players have no access to any technique or weapon, meaning the only way to deal with it is either X's charged shots and Zero's jumping slash to hit Eregion's torso. Most of Eregion attacks can be easily avoided by dashing at the right moment (in case of the swoop, players must dash in the same direction Eregion is headed), except for the shot barrage, which requires a well-timed dash jump over it.

Defeating Eregion unlocks the stage map, allowing players to choose their next level.

\subsection{Web Spider}\label{boss:Web_spider}
Originally a Maverick Hunter of the 0th Special Unit led by Zero, Web Spider later joined Repliforce, where he conducted several successful campaigns, eventually becoming the leader of their guerrilla unit~\cite{wayback:X4_resources}. During Repliforce's coup, Spider and his unit were tasked with guarding Repliforce's secret beam cannon hidden in the jungle. Dedicated to his mission, Spider ignored X/Zero's demand to disarm, leading to their confrontation, which proved fatal for him.


Web Spider relies on only two primary attacks to damage the player. However, these attacks inflict high damage and can be chained together effectively, posing a significant challenge. This is particularly true if players tackle this stage early, as they may have limited HP. During the first phase of the battle, Web Spider frequently retreats offscreen by climbing his web, reappearing to attack with his \emph{Lightning Web}. This homing electric web slowly pursues the player and traps them upon contact. While trapped, the web deals continuous damage until the player breaks free or a set damage threshold is reached. After firing the web, Spider quickly retreats offscreen again. Occasionally, if the player becomes trapped, Spider may descend early, landing on X/Zero to deal additional damage.

When Web Spider's health is reduced to half, the second phase begins. Spider descends and creates a large, non-damaging web spanning the screen. From this position, he alternates between two attacks, the\emph{Lightning Web}, the same as in the first phase, and the \emph{Baby Spider Attack}, which summons four mini spiders that crawl along the floor and walls, obstructing the player's movement and, in X's case, often travel below the regular buster's line of fire, requiring a charged shot to eliminate them.
During this phase, Spider remains on-screen, increasing the opportunities to attack him. However, his attack rate also rises, and his large body becomes a significant threat due to contact damage and the unpredictable movement while on the web.

Dealing with Web Spider requires careful movement and precise attacks. During Phase 1, players should focus on attacking Spider as he descends while avoiding the Lightning Web. For X, charged shots are effective for maintaining distance, while Zero must rely on jump slashes, which are riskier due to Spider's proximity and the risk of getting cough in the web. In Phase 2 Spider becomes more vulnerable but also more aggressive. Players should exploit his increased on-screen presence to deal consistent damage while avoiding his attacks, X's being advantaged due the charged shots helping to clean up baby spiders and damaging Spider simultaneously, while Zero must focus on avoiding being overwhelmed by their movements.Should player already face Web Spider after having defeated Slash Beast, X's Twin Slasher and Zero's Shippuuga will deal increased damage to Spider, although in Zero's case using this weapon is relatively ineffective due to Spider's position. X's Twin Slasher, on the other hand, proves to be incredibly effective, as using it during the first phase on Spider's web will cut it, causing him to fall and take even more damage. 

Upon defeating Web Spider, X will gain access to the Lightning Web~(\ref{Lightning_Web}), while Zero will learn the Raijingeki~(\ref{Raijingeki}).
